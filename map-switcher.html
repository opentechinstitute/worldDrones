Countries below are sorted into three groups: those who possess unmanned drones, those who possess armed unmanned drones, and those who use these drones in combat.

IsraelIsrael has domestic production of drones and imports drone engines from the United Kingdom. Israel is the largest exporter of drones in the world, responsible for 41 percent of all drones exported between 2001 and 2011, according to a database compiled by the Stockholm International Peace Research Institute, though it refuses to release the full list of countries to which it has sold military arms. A partial list of recipients includes the U.S., U.K., Canada, France, Australia, Germany, Spain, Brazil, India, China, Poland, the Netherlands, Azerbaijan, and Nigeria. ChinaChina has domestic production of drones, and limited imports from Israel. There are limited imports from Japan, too, and some reports indicate that Germany has provided the engines used in Chinese drones. Another report alleges that Iran gave China access to the RQ-170 drone that went down in China. In August 2006, Japan's Yamaha Motor Company was accused of selling the RMAX helicopter drones to the Beijing Technology Company, which has ties to the Chinese People's Liberation Army (PLA), in violation of Japan's Foreign Exchange and Foreign Trade Control Law. Chinese authorities said in February 2013 that they had considered using a drone to kill a drug dealer in Myanmar who was responsible for the deaths of 13 Chinese soldiers. China also recorded the successful flight of its first stealth combat drone, the "Sharp Sword," in November 2013. China unveiled the CH-4 and Yilong drones equipped with Blue Arrow-7 anti-tank missiles, which have designs similar to that of the U.S. Predator drones equipped with Hellfire missiles.[xxv] China's drone Arsenal has grown large enough to compete with the United States. Reports accuse China of hacking into U.S. drone companies and accessing information needed to ramp up their drone technologies.YesYesYes

France has joined with Germany, Greece, Italy, Netherlands, Poland, and Spain to create a _drone users club" intended to compete with Israeli and U.S. drones markets. IranIran has domestic production of drones. In August 2010, Iran unveiled the _Karar" drone, which President Mahmoud Ahmadinejad called an _ambassador of death," and which reportedly can carry up to four cruise missiles with a range of over 600 miles. Iranian-made Ababil drones (which function more as cruise missiles) have been used by Hezbollah. According to the Iranian military, there is also domestic production of drones of short-range reconnaissance such as the Yasseer drones and Shahad-129 drones capable of 1,700 km range carrying up to 8 missiles. In November 2013, they released Fotros drones with a 2,000 km range.[xlvi] Officials claimed to have reproduced a U.S. drones that was downed in Iran.YesYes?
